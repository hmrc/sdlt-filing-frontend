Do you want to pay SDLT on the market value or only on the percentage of the property you are buying?

Error: {{state.validationMessage('paySDLT')}}
You can choose to pay SDLT on the market value of the property. This is known as a ‘market value election’.
How does this work?

First Time Buyers Relief will be applied to the SDLT on:

  • the market value of the property, or
  • on the market value of the share of the property that is ultimately obtainable under the scheme

No further SDLT will be due if you purchase further shares in the property or acquire the property outright.

Alternatively you can choose to pay SDLT on the amount you are paying for your initial purchase.

How does this work?

If you pay SDLT in stages, your First Time Buyers Relief will only be applied to the initial payment you make, including any rental payments.

Further payments towards your property purchase made at a later stage may be liable to SDLT which could add up to a higher or lower total amount payable than the ‘market value election’ option. First Time Buyers Relief is not available against those further payments.

However if your total share in the property is never more than 80%, then you will not be liable for any further SDLT.
